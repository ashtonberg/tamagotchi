Ashton's Knockoff Tamagotchi Project

Overview

This is just a very, very quickly put together overview of what the final product of my project looked like. The home screen shows your pet in a jumping animation, a lightbulb on the right side of the screen, and four selectable options at the top of the screen. The four options at the top are feed, toggle light, stats, and game.

The goal is to just not let your pet die. If its hunger of happiness reaches zero, a "Pet Died" screen will show up and it's game over. If the pet's happiness or hunger is below half, an attention circle will be displayed in the bottom right of the screen.

While on the home screen, the green button moves the position of the cursor to the right one, with wrap-around to the left. Pressing the blue button will select the option currently pointed to by the cursor.

Selecting the 'feed' option will make an apple pop up in front of the pet for a second. If the pet is hungry, a heart will pop up for a second to the right of them, and their hunger will go up a little. If they are full, an 'X' will pop up instead, and their happiness will drop a little.

Selecting the 'light' option toggles the light on the right side of the screen. The pet likes having the light on, so if it is off for too long, they will start losing happiness. However, the pet falls asleep every couple of minutes, and you need to turn the light off before they wake up or their happiness will go down.

Selecting the 'stats' option opens up a stats menu that displays three separate bars for hunger, happiness, and level. These represent the pet's current state. You want their hunger and happiness bars to be full. The level bar just shows how long they have been alive for.

Selecting the 'game' option will enter you into a minigame and a song (lostwoods from Zelda) will begin playing. The game plays for three rounds, and the current round is shown at the top. The goal is to guess which direction the pet will turn. The green button will guesses left, and the blue button guesses right. Once you make your selection, the pet will choose a random direction to turn. If they turn in the direction you guessed, a heart will pop up. If they turn in the opposite direction, an 'X' will pop up. After playing for three rounds, the pet's happiness will go up based on how many guesses you got correct, you will return to the home screen, and the music will turn off.

If you twist the potentiometer dial while pressing the yellow button, you can adjust the brightness of the display. Turning the potentiometer while not holding down the yellow button won't do anything.

You can connect to the device over bluetooth. I used the LightBlue app for communicating with it. While connected, if you send a hex value ending in 0xAB, it's supposed to simulate "petting" your pet, and a heart will pop up in the bottom left of the screen. Sending any other value won't do anything. If the pet's happiness or hunger drop below half while connected to a device, then it will send a notification to your device.
